Churn Prediction for Fundraising Organization

1. Situation

The organization, a fundraising entity, was concerned about donor attrition and its impact on revenue. They provided a dataset on March 2, 2020, to build a model that could predict which donors were most likely to churn by October 25, 2020. Accurate predictions would enable targeted retention efforts, helping to minimize losses and maintain donor engagement.

2. Task

My task was to create a predictive model that could accurately identify the top 20% of donors most likely to churn. This involved developing a unified dataset (basetable) from multiple sources, selecting appropriate features, and comparing the performance of various machine learning models, including Artificial Neural Networks (ANN), Random Forest, and Logistic Regression.

3. Action

To achieve the objectives, I carried out the following steps:

  • Data Preparation: Merged multiple tables, engineered features (e.g., frequency, recency, monetary value), and handled missing data through imputation.
  • Modeling: Built and trained models using Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and ANN. Each model was evaluated based on its ability to predict donor churn, with a particular focus on the top 20% most likely to churn.
  • Model Evaluation: Performance metrics such as accuracy, precision, and recall were used to assess the models. The ANN model was found to be the most effective, achieving over 80% accuracy.
4. Result

The final ANN model identified the top 20% of donors most likely to churn with over 80% accuracy. This model led to a targeted retention campaign that resulted in a 60% retention gain among the high-risk donors, effectively reducing donor attrition and increasing the organizationâ€™s fundraising potential.
